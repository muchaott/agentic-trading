    Risk treatment

    Guardrails

    • Results may be delayed, revised, incomplete, or affected by survivorship bias.
    • No guarantee of future performance or suitability for any investor.
    • Conflicts and data rights must be reviewed before paid access.

    Trust architecture

    Methodology is part of the product, not a footer.

    The MVP presents constrained, versioned research artifacts. It does not let users optimize parameters, mine arbitrary tickers, or convert backtests into live advice.

    Strategy versioning

    Every report carries a strategy rule version, methodology version, data source snapshot, disclosure version, and review timestamp.

    Performance presentation

    CAGR, drawdown, volatility, benchmark comparison, trade count, and fee/slippage assumptions are shown together. APY and return leaderboards are intentionally absent.

    Backtest constraints

    Users can switch approved timeframes, benchmarks, and gross versus estimated net display. They cannot tune strategy thresholds or search every ETF.

    Launch gates

    Paid signals stay deferred until legal posture, data rights, disclosure templates, conflicts policy, and methodology review are complete.

    Required language

    Research-only disclosure pattern

    Historical or hypothetical performance is not a guarantee of future results. Research events are educational artifacts and are not personalized investment advice. Data may be delayed, revised, or incomplete. Trading involves risk, including loss of principal.

    Disclosures and controls

    Data, assumptions, risk, and conflicts stay visible.

    Strategy Ledger treats source quality, methodology limits, and conflict review as product surfaces. These controls are shown before any future subscription work.

    Data sources

    MVP research runs use versioned adjusted historical ETF prices and fixed universe snapshots. Redistribution rights, vendor terms, and revision handling must be approved before launch.

    Performance assumptions

    Backtests are precomputed with documented sample windows, execution timing, benchmark, transaction-cost, and slippage assumptions. Estimated net results are illustrative.

    Risk language

    Trading ETFs can lose money, including principal. Historical and hypothetical results do not predict future returns, and drawdowns may exceed modeled results.

    Conflicts

    Founder, affiliate, or employee trading in covered ETFs requires policy review, disclosure, and incident handling before any paid product gate opens.

    Artifact ledger

    Every performance page must carry these references

    • Strategy rule version
    • Backtest engine version
    • Data source snapshot
    • Assumptions version
    • Disclosure version
    • Reviewer and approval timestamp
